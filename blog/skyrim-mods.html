My fondness for Skyrim mods

  • Vincent DEVINE
  • 08/08/2023
What is Skyrim ?

Skyrim, also known by its full title "The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim," is an open-world role-playing game developed by Bethesda Game Studios and published by Bethesda Softworks.
Released in 2011, the game is set in a vast medieval-fantasy world where players take on the role of the "Dovahkiin," a prophetic hero gifted with the power to wield the "Thu'um," ancient shouts capable of influencing reality.
Skyrim offers free exploration, epic quests, diverse characters, and the ability to customize one's character based on gameplay preferences.

What is a mod ?

A mod, short for "modification," refers to a creation made by players or third-party developers to alter, enhance, or add content to an existing video game.
These modifications can encompass new characters, items, levels, features, or even changes to graphics and gameplay.
Mods provide a dynamic way to explore and expand a game's universe, enabling players to personalize their experience and discover new creative perspectives.

With a game that is now 12 years old, many aspects could be refreshed considering technological advancements. However, its captivating story and exceptional map remain.
Thanks to its immense success and thriving community, Skyrim has turned into a true playground for modders.
Indeed, the "Special Edition" version now boasts an impressive collection of over 70,000 mods, and that's only on Nexus Mods.

I advise you to play the game at least once without mods to experience the original gameplay. This will help you identify what you want to modify or experiment with for your future adventures.

My recommendations

Here is a list of my recommendations for mods. However, consider this as a foundation, and I encourage you to customize it according to your preferences.
Feel free to be curious and explore what's available in this field.

Firstly, I recommend looking into the work of the Skyrim Guild (Skyrim Guild) and their guides. By following their instructions, you'll be able to set up numerous modifications.

Regarding retexture mods, I suggest the following:
- Frankly for armors
- Rustic for everyday items
- Bijin for NPCs (Non-Player Characters)
- Kajuan for creatures
- Mari for plants
- JS for objects
- JK's for locations and cities

Then, add an ENB following its prerequisites to achieve a very appealing graphical rendering (personally, I use Rudy ENB).
An ENB (Enhanced Natural Beauty) is a graphical mod for Skyrim, similar to shaders in Minecraft. It enhances graphics by adjusting lighting, depth of field, colors, and visual effects.

While I'm less focused on mods adding quests, there are nevertheless several interesting mods in this regard (and I know they have a part in the guide).

Concerning armor and weapons, I'll let you make your choices based on your preferences.

For NPC (Non-Player Characters), you could consider:
- My Home is Your Home (allows designating homes for NPCs)
- Rich Skyrim Merchants (for wealthier merchants)

For housing, you could consider:
- Elysium Estate (my favorite, moderately sized with numerous spaces and a Skyforge)
- If you're playing as a female, the Female Mannequins

Regarding the User Interface (UI), I recommend:
- SkyUI
- A Quality World Map

If you're seeking a completely new experience and have a powerful system, I highly recommend the Nolvus mod pack

These recommendations are just a starting point
I encourage you to adapt them based on your personal preferences. Don't hesitate to be curious and explore more in this exciting realm of Skyrim mods.
